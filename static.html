Chawa (2020) is a Marathi-language historical drama film directed by Rajendra Raut. The film is a portrayal of the Maratha Empire's struggles, focusing on the life of Chandrasen, a brave warrior who plays a significant role in defending the Maratha Empire and battling against external forces. The movie is set in the backdrop of the rise of the Maratha Empire and emphasizes themes of loyalty, valor, and the warrior spirit.

Plot Overview: Chawa tells the story of Chandrasen, a warrior in the Maratha Empire who, despite being a lesser-known historical figure, is depicted as a symbol of bravery and sacrifice. The film follows his journey, starting from his early days as a soldier to becoming an important figure in the Maratha army. The central theme of the film revolves around Chandrasen's loyalty to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and his commitment to the Maratha cause. It showcases his participation in key military campaigns and battles, where his strategic mind and bravery are put to the test. The movie emphasizes how Chandrasen’s personal life and ambitions intersect with his duty to the empire, highlighting the emotional and moral dilemmas he faces as a warrior. The title of the film, Chawa (meaning “a warrior” or "hero"), underscores the heroic nature of the protagonist's character, representing his courage, leadership, and sacrifice for the Maratha cause.

Mangesh Desai as Chandrasen: Mangesh Desai plays the protagonist, Chandrasen, a warrior in the Maratha Empire. His performance captures the essence of a fierce and loyal soldier who is committed to defending his land and king. Medha Manjrekar as Chandrasen’s wife: Medha Manjrekar portrays the emotional anchor in Chandrasen's life, showing the sacrifices that come with being the spouse of a warrior. She represents the strength of the families left behind while warriors go off to fight. Makarand Anaspure as Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj: Makarand Anaspure appears as Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, and his role highlights the relationship between Shivaji Maharaj and his warriors. His leadership and vision for the Maratha Empire serve as a guiding force for characters like Chandrasen. Nandita Patkar and other supporting actors play key roles that bring emotional depth and historical context to the film. Their performances contribute to the portrayal of the Maratha military structure and the social dynamics of the time.

Valor and Loyalty: Like many historical films about the Maratha Empire, Chawa emphasizes the themes of valor, duty, and loyalty to the king. Chandrasen’s unwavering commitment to his duties as a soldier in the Maratha army is at the heart of the film. Warrior Spirit: The film focuses on the life of a warrior, illustrating not only the battles fought on the front lines but also the emotional and personal struggles that soldiers face. It portrays the difficulties and sacrifices warriors made in defense of their land and their king. Historical Struggles: The film explores the broader context of the Maratha Empire’s rise, their battles against the Mughals, and their constant struggle for independence and sovereignty. It sheds light on the importance of lesser-known heroes and their contributions to the Maratha cause. Emotional Depth: In addition to the action-packed war sequences, Chawa also highlights the emotional depth of the characters, particularly the personal sacrifices they make for the greater good. It delves into the relationships between warriors and their families, exploring how those left behind endure the uncertainty and loss associated with war.

Filming and Production: The production of Chawa involved significant research into the historical period to ensure that the costumes, weapons, and sets were authentic to the 17th century. The filmmakers aimed to depict the Maratha Empire's culture, architecture, and military practices with historical accuracy. The movie includes action sequences and battle scenes, which required careful choreography to create the intensity and realism of warfare. The filmmakers made use of outdoor locations in Maharashtra to replicate the landscapes and fortifications of the Maratha Empire, ensuring that the movie’s visual elements felt grounded in the real historical setting.

Reception: Chawa was appreciated by audiences, particularly for its focus on a lesser-known hero from Maratha history. The performances were praised, with special mention of Mangesh Desai’s portrayal of Chandrasen, which brought a sense of authenticity and emotional depth to the character. While the film may not have had a massive mainstream reach, it resonated strongly with those interested in the history of the Maratha Empire and its unsung warriors.

The movie serves as a tribute to the brave warriors who fought for the Maratha Empire but did not always receive the recognition they deserved. By focusing on a lesser-known figure like Chandrasen, Chawa brings attention to the wide array of individuals who contributed to the success of the Maratha forces and provides a more comprehensive view of the Maratha military structure.
