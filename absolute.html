Fatteshikast

Fatteshikast is a 2019 Marathi-language historical drama film directed by Amit P. H. The movie is set in the 17th century and focuses on the life and struggles of the Maratha warrior Tanaji Malusare, a key figure in the Maratha Empire's military history, particularly known for his role in the Battle of Sinhagad. While the movie shares a similar backdrop to Tanhaji: The Unsung Warrior, it provides a unique portrayal of the Maratha hero and his contributions.

The film is centered around the events leading to the Battle of Sinhagad, where Tanaji Malusare (played by Ankush Chaudhari) played a crucial role in recapturing the Kondhana Fort from the Mughals. The battle was one of the most significant confrontations during the Maratha Empire's rise, and the story depicts Tanaji's courage, valor, and sacrifice, as well as the challenges he faced in his personal life and his devotion to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

The movie also explores the political and social dynamics of the time, as well as the tactical brilliance of Tanaji and his army in defeating the Mughal forces. It presents a perspective from the Maratha side, showing the cultural and emotional connections to the fort and the warriors who fought for it.

Unique Aspects of the Film: Historical Focus: While Tanhaji (2020) focused on the more mainstream, large-scale action spectacle, Fatteshikast focused on a more detailed and dramatic portrayal of Tanaji's life and his journey to recapture Kondhana Fort. Authenticity: The movie aimed to present a realistic depiction of the era, with attention to period costumes, set designs, and historical details. Production: The film was made with a relatively smaller budget compared to its Bollywood counterpart but managed to capture the essence of the period with strong performances and a solid narrative.

Fatteshikast received praise for its attempt to tell the story of Tanaji Malusare, which was not as widely known compared to other legendary figures in Indian history. While the film may not have had the same pan-Indian appeal as Tanhaji, it garnered positive feedback, especially in Maharashtra, where it resonated strongly with local audiences due to its connection with the Maratha identity. Key Filming Locations: Maharashtra Forts: Several scenes were shot at real historical forts in Maharashtra to maintain authenticity. The most significant among them was the Sinhagad Fort, where the actual Battle of Sinhagad took place. This fort is a crucial part of Maratha history and was an essential location for the film to stay true to the historical events.

Set Construction: While the film featured historical forts and real-life locations, many scenes were shot on specially constructed sets. The filmmakers created large, detailed sets to depict the palaces, battlefields, and the environment around the Maratha Empire. These sets helped bring the historical era to life and were designed with careful attention to the period's architecture, costume, and weaponry.

Outdoor Locations: Since the movie emphasizes warfare and battle scenes, outdoor locations in the Maharashtra countryside were used. The rugged landscapes, which are representative of the geographical features around the forts, added to the movie's authentic look. These locations helped convey the strategic importance of fortifications in the Maratha Empire.

Battle Sequences: A significant portion of the film focuses on intense action and battle scenes, particularly the climactic battle for Kondhana Fort (Sinhagad). These sequences required meticulous planning and choreographed stunts, involving large groups of extras portraying soldiers.

Costumes and Props: To capture the historical period, great effort went into designing period-accurate costumes, weapons, and armor. The filmmakers worked closely with historians and costume designers to recreate the attire of the Maratha warriors and Mughal soldiers accurately. The weapons, particularly swords and shields, were designed to reflect the era's actual military equipment.

Direction and Cinematography: Director Amit P. H. focused on delivering an immersive experience, utilizing close-up shots of the characters during emotional moments and wide-angle shots for the grand battle sequences. Cinematographer Nirav Shah contributed significantly to the film's visual appeal, capturing the beauty of the landscapes and the intensity of the war scenes.

Ankush Chaudhari as Tanaji Malusare: Ankush Chaudhari plays the central character of Tanaji Malusare, a legendary Maratha warrior known for his role in capturing the Kondhana Fort (later renamed Sinhagad Fort) from the Mughal forces. His portrayal of Tanaji captures the warrior's determination, bravery, and loyalty to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Madhavi Juvekar as Savitribai Malusare: Madhavi Juvekar plays Tanaji’s wife, Savitribai, who supports her husband throughout his journey. Her character shows the emotional strength of Tanaji's family, particularly the sacrifices made in times of war. Sayaji Shinde as Udaybhan Rathore: Sayaji Shinde portrays the antagonist, Udaybhan Rathore, a ruthless Rajput officer in the Mughal army, who defends the Kondhana Fort. His character represents the Mughal forces' challenge to the Marathas during the battle.

Sharad Kelkar as Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj: Sharad Kelkar plays Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the legendary Maratha king who inspires and supports Tanaji in his mission. Shivaji Maharaj’s leadership and valor are central to the film’s narrative, especially in motivating his warriors. Vishal Nikam as Baji Prabhu Deshpande: Vishal Nikam plays the role of Baji Prabhu Deshpande, another Maratha warrior who is also significant in Maratha history. He is known for his bravery and tactical support in the Maratha empire’s military campaigns.
